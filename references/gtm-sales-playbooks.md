# AI-Native GTM and Sales Playbooks

Use this reference when the user asks about go-to-market, sales, business development, partnerships, pipeline, account planning, meeting prep, forecast risk, stalled deals, or customer follow-up.

The principle:

```text
Founder owns relationship strategy and judgment.
AI assembles context, drafts first-pass artifacts, separates sourced facts from inference, and creates next-step options.
```

Do not invent commitments, customer facts, dates, stakeholders, budgets, or relationship context. If the user does not provide source material, request it or produce a source-needed checklist.

## 1. Pipeline Prioritization from Underworked Accounts

Use when a founder or sales team has a broad list of accounts, leads, communities, partners, or warm contacts and needs to decide where to focus.

Inputs:
- account list or segment
- CRM export or spreadsheet
- notes, transcripts, email threads, Slack or chat context
- usage signals, support signals, product events
- GTM updates or account pages

Output:
- ranked account list
- trigger rationale
- stakeholder map
- next-best action
- outreach sequence
- CRM-ready next steps
- sourced facts vs inferred opportunity

Ranking criteria:
- trigger
- pain
- stakeholder access
- urgency
- strategic value
- likelihood of a concrete next step

Founder-only decisions:
- Which accounts deserve personal attention.
- Which relationships should not be automated.
- Which opportunities fit the company wedge.

## 2. Meeting Prep and Follow-Up

Use before and after customer, partner, investor, renewal, or enterprise meetings.

Inputs:
- calendar context
- account notes
- prior calls
- email threads
- usage/support context
- open workstreams
- post-meeting notes or transcript

Output:
- meeting brief
- goals
- customer context
- likely priorities
- risks and open asks
- questions to ask
- proposed asks
- follow-up email
- internal recap
- CRM-ready update

Guardrail:
- If post-meeting notes do not exist, stop after the prep brief and list what to capture after the meeting.

## 3. Forecast Review and Commit Risk

Use when the founder or GTM leader needs to decide whether a deal should stay in commit, move to upside, or be pulled.

Inputs:
- forecast snapshot
- opportunity records
- call notes
- email threads
- legal, security, procurement, or support status
- usage signals
- owner notes

Output:
- commit / upside / pull recommendation
- sourced facts
- inferred risks
- deal-by-deal rationale
- owner follow-ups
- missing proof required to keep confidence

Founder-only decisions:
- Whether to trust the forecast.
- Whether to escalate, discount, pause, or requalify.

## 4. Strategic Account Plan Refresh

Use when an important account plan is stale or the team needs a current account strategy pack.

Inputs:
- account record
- opportunity record
- recent calls
- account threads
- customer emails
- usage notes
- product needs
- prior account plans
- company context

Output:
- stakeholder map
- discovery gaps
- risks
- value hypothesis
- objections
- proof points
- next-best actions
- stale information and assumptions needing owner review

Skill / memory opportunity:
- Account planning Skill for recurring enterprise, partner, or strategic customer motion.

## 5. Stalled Deal Diagnosis

Use when a deal, pilot, partnership, or expansion motion is stuck.

Inputs:
- stage history
- closed activities
- call transcripts
- email threads
- internal deal threads
- security/legal/procurement notes
- account context

Output:
- blocker classification
- prior attempts summary
- missing information
- internal expert or asset needed
- customer-facing next step
- internal escalation plan
- sourced facts vs inferred blocker

Blocker classes:
- no urgent pain
- no economic buyer
- no clear next step
- procurement/legal/security
- product gap
- weak champion
- internal priority mismatch
- unclear ROI

## 6. Business Development / Partnership Opportunity Map

Use when the motion is not direct sales: partners, channels, integrations, communities, investors, ecosystem relationships, or strategic allies.

Inputs:
- target partner list
- ecosystem map
- intro paths
- mutual value hypothesis
- prior conversations
- partner assets
- integration or co-marketing possibilities

Output:
- partner prioritization
- mutual value hypothesis
- stakeholder map
- first ask
- proof required
- follow-up sequence
- risks and asymmetries

Founder-only decisions:
- Whether the partnership advances the core wedge or distracts from it.
- Whether trust, brand, or strategic control makes this human-led.

## GTM Failure Modes

- AI writes polished outreach without a real account trigger.
- Pipeline looks active because artifacts exist, not because customers are moving.
- Founder delegates relationship judgment too early.
- Account plans become summaries instead of decisions.
- Forecast confidence is inferred from optimism rather than sourced customer evidence.
- GTM data stays scattered across notes, email, transcripts, and spreadsheets, so every review starts from zero.

Countermeasure:
- Every GTM artifact must separate sourced facts from inference and end with owner-reviewed next actions.
